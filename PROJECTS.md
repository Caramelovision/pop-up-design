## üí° Inspiration Sources

### Classic Techniques
- **Parallel folds:** Creating stable bases for standing elements
- **V-folds:** Dynamic angled pop-ups that catch light beautifully
- **Rotating elements:** Adding movement with tabs and sliders
- **Layered depth:** Multiple paper layers for dimension

### Contemporary Inspirations
- Japanese origami and paper cutting traditions
- Contemporary paper artists like Kiri Ken and Anselmo Alliegro
- Paper engineering in luxury packaging and advertising
- Installation art and sculptural approaches to paper

### Theme Ideas
1. **Seasonal Collections:** Spring flowers, autumn leaves, winter wonderlands, summer scenes
2. **Cultural Celebrations:** Diwali lights, Christmas nativity, Day of the Dead altars, Chinese New Year
3. **Fantasy & Mythology:** Dragon pop-ups, fairy tale scenes, magical landscapes
4. **Architecture:** Iconic buildings, bridges, historical landmarks
5. **Nature & Wildlife:** Animal habitats, underwater scenes, forest canopies
6. **Abstract & Experimental:** Non-representational designs, kinetic sculptures, light-interactive pieces

## üìã Project Planning Checklist

When starting a new pop-up project:
- [ ] Define target audience and purpose
- [ ] Research existing similar designs
- [ ] Create detailed sketches and blueprints
- [ ] Plan material requirements
- [ ] Build prototype versions
- [ ] Test durability and functionality
- [ ] Refine based on testing results
- [ ] Finalize production design
- [ ] Create assembly instructions if needed
- [ ] Document process with photos/videos
